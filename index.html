The grid's problem

Britain's wind and solar capacity is growing faster than the grid can carry the power. In 2025, over £1 billion was paid to generators to switch off turbines. The electricity existed. The demand existed. The infrastructure to connect them did not.

The datacentre's problem

Datacentres are buying electricity at whatever price the market charges, at whatever time they need it. The batteries already on site — UPS systems, backup power — sit fully charged and idle, waiting for a grid failure that statistically almost never comes. They are an energy asset being used as insurance.

The link nobody has made

A datacentre that charges its batteries during a renewable surplus and discharges during a peak is performing exactly the function the grid is paying billions to avoid needing. The market exists. The infrastructure exists. The software to connect them has not, until now.

Battery response

Software that turns your batteries into a trading desk.

VoltPilot operates entirely on the energy side of your operation. It never touches compute, never adjusts workloads, never affects uptime.

No new hardware. No operational risk. Software only.

1. Learns your site

VoltPilot ingests your historical consumption patterns, battery specifications, and site constraints. It builds a model of your flexibility — how much you can shift, for how long, within your operational limits.

2. Reads the market

The platform monitors half-hourly electricity prices, renewable generation forecasts, and NESO flexibility market signals in real time. It identifies the windows where buying power is cheapest and where selling flexibility pays most.

3. Acts automatically

VoltPilot schedules battery charge and discharge cycles to minimise your electricity costs, enters bids into NESO markets when the opportunity warrants, and generates auditable reports on every action taken. You approve the strategy. The system executes it.

Connected across multiple sites, VoltPilot creates something no single datacentre can access alone: a virtual power plant. Power Haven manages this aggregation without exchanging any operational or commercial data between operators.

Where we are going.

Today, VoltPilot is software. It connects existing batteries to existing markets and makes both work better. That is the immediate value, and it is real.

But the infrastructure problem runs deeper than trading strategy. Power Haven's long-term purpose is to develop co-located renewable generation and battery assets at or near datacentre sites — creating infrastructure that is genuinely self-sufficient and genuinely grid-positive.

Now

Software platform connecting existing on-site batteries to UK electricity markets. Automated trading. NESO market participation. Multi-site aggregation.

Next

Co-developed battery infrastructure at partner sites, expanding the flexibility pool and improving market participation economics for both Power Haven and the operator.

Future

Renewable generation assets — solar, wind — developed in proximity to datacentre clusters, creating a direct supply relationship between clean generation and digital demand.
